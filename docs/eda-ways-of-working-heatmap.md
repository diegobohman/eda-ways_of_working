# ED&A Ways of Working — Heatmap

Purpose

- Provide a lightweight, perception‑based visual artifact that surfaces recurring ways‑of‑working tensions across Flight Levels (Strategy / Coordination / Team).
- Stimulate system-level conversations and clarify ownership for follow-up actions without ranking or judging teams.

When to use

- During cross-team forums (All Units, Platform Healthcheck, Strategy Agenda) to quickly surface patterns.
- As a conversation input during retrospectives or leadership alignment sessions when discussing systemic constraints.

Design principles

- Not a maturity model — shows perceived tension, not performance.
- Psychological safety first — visibility, not judgment.
- System‑owned — teams and leaders co-own the map and resulting actions.
- Lightweight and reversible — treat the map as an experimentable artifact.

Structure (initial)

- X axis: Ways‑of‑working themes (suggested starters): Outcomes clarity, Decision ownership, Flow of work, Learning loops, Dependency management, Measurement & KPIs.
- Y axis: Flight Levels — Strategy / Coordination / Team.
- Cells: Perceived tension (Green = minimal/recently resolved; Yellow = recurring; Red = persistent/high concern).
- Notes column: short text for the evidence or example that motivated the rating and suggested next owner.

Simple template (example)

| Flight Level | Theme | Heat | Example / Evidence | Suggested owner |
|---|---:|---:|---|---|
| Strategy | Outcomes clarity | Yellow | Multiple teams report unclear KPIs for Q2 initiative | Strategy owner / Exec sponsor |
| Coordination | Dependency management | Red | Teams blocked waiting for shared data pipeline | Platform lead |
| Team | Learning loops | Green | Regular experiments and show-and-tells in Platform team | Unit lead |

How to use (steps / cadence)

1. Select the context (All Units, Platform Healthcheck, Strategy Agenda, or targeted leadership forum).
2. Invite a cross-section of participants (unit leads, platform leads, 1-2 leadership reps). 3–7 people is a good starting size.
3. For each theme at the relevant Flight Level, ask participants to state whether they have observed recurring tension (Green/Yellow/Red) and add a short note explaining why.
4. Capture the map (screenshot / archived markdown) and list A) suggested owner(s) for follow-up and B) one next experiment or decision to run.
5. Review outcomes in the next cadence (e.g., next All Units or next Strategy Agenda) and update the map.

Roles involved

- Facilitator: runs the session and ensures psychological safety (rotates between DSO coach, Platform Healthcheck lead, or unit lead).
- Contributors: unit leads, platform leads, product owners, leadership reps as needed.
- Owner(s) for follow-up: assigned per tension (can be a team lead, platform lead, or a leader responsible for coordination).

Expected outputs

- A captured heatmap artifact (markdown or image) with short evidence notes per red/yellow cell.
- A concise list of owners + 1 next experiment/decision per significant tension.
- A short report-back item for the next forum describing progress or blocking constraints.

Success signals / measures

- Artifact is used as conversation input in at least one cross-team forum within the pilot cycle.
- At least 60% of red/yellow items have an assigned owner and a next step documented.
- Participants report increased clarity on ownership and fewer recurring surprises in follow-ups.

Links & related

- Related Issue: [#5](https://github.com/diegobohman/eda-ways_of_working/issues/5)
- Related concepts: Flight Levels, Dynamic Shared Ownership (DSO), Strategy Agenda.

How to roll this out (adoption note)

1. Pilot with one forum (e.g., upcoming Strategy Agenda or Platform Healthcheck).
2. Use facilitator script (one-paragraph) to introduce the artifact and reassure on psychological safety.
3. Capture pilot learnings and iterate the themes and template.
4. If useful, create a small `experiments/heatmap-pilot/` folder to track pilot hypothesis, evidence, and learnings.
