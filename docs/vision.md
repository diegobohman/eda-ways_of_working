# Vision
## Project name
DSO like ways of working for Enterprise Data & Analytics (ED&A) platform
## Purpose
This project is about the ways of working of the ED&A platform. Ways of working include:
- Ways of decision making
- Ways of how to prioritize
- Setting strategic priorities
- Keeping track and adjusting work or strategic priorities
- Meetings to keep the value chain going
- How to measure success

The idea is to use this project to co-create necessary interaction formats and artifacts (e.g. KPI definitions) and documenting them in Markdown files. Through github issues we want to document if something is not working and then co-create improvements

Our ways of working should follow the DSO (Dynamic Shared Ownership) principles:
- Focused on a mission to deliver beneficial outcomes for all
- Collaborate in a network of entrepreneurial teams
- ... to co-create new value through reimagining the system ...
- ... by executing, exploring and learning in rapid cycles
- Enabled by being our whole authentic selves in creative mindset

## Problem to solve
Currently many people are very busy in implementing ED&A demands and either don't want to or don't have enough time to improve our ways of working. Also the DSO ideas are often seen as rather confusing or disturbing and not being helpful. Maybe also a notion of either we do DSO or we don't rather than figuring out where which DSO practices are helpful and where other practices are helpful. Also the context we live in, be it the way people are being incentivised, signals IT leadership and ED&A leadership is sending either enables or restricts more helpful ways of working. Another problem we are facing is that only a small group of people (e.g. platform lead and BizDevOps lead) try to improve ways of working and often do it without involving the affected people. This leads to resistance.

## Desired outcome
This project should somehow enable the creation of a shared map that helps everyone who is involved to see our ways of working components and to which degree they are helpful. This should enable a co-creation in identifying which ways of working need to be improved and which purpose they serve.

## Success indicators
- ways of working are not just owned by a few roles but by everyone
- there is less complaining and more co-creation of ways of working solutions
- we all adopt a continuous test and learn mindset
- not only do we co-create ways of working improvements but we also enact our new ways of working together (e.g. it's not just the BizDevOps lead who facilitates new meeting formats)

## Evolution of the ED\&A Ways of Working

This project is intentionally designed to **evolve in stages**, allowing ED\&A teams to engage at the level that is useful for them, while continuously learning what actually works in our context.

### Phase 1 – Make the Implicit Explicit (Transparency & Orientation)

**Goal:** Create a shared understanding of how we *currently* work.

*   Capture existing ways of working as they are today (not as they “should be”)
*   Describe current decision-making, prioritization, governance, and meeting formats
*   Document tensions, pain points, and contradictions without trying to solve them
*   Establish a shared language around ED\&A ways of working and DSO principles

**Artifacts**

*   Markdown documents describing current practices. Those markdown documents are part of this Github repository and should live in a meaningful folder structure.
*   Lightweight maps of decision flows and responsibilities
*   GitHub issues used primarily for surfacing tensions and friction

**Mindset**

> “First, let’s see the system we’re actually operating in.”


### Phase 2 – Sense & Evaluate What Helps (Collective Reflection)

**Goal:** Build shared awareness of what is helpful, harmful, or outdated.

*   Invite broad participation in commenting, refining, and challenging documented practices
*   Use Github issues and discussions to explore *why* something isn’t working
*   Separate **purpose** of a practice from its **current implementation**
*   Explicitly acknowledge trade-offs (e.g. control vs speed, alignment vs autonomy)

**Artifacts**

*   Commented documents with contextual notes
*   Issues framed as learning questions, not improvement tickets
*   Early heuristics (e.g. “works well when…”, “causes friction when…”)

**Mindset**

> “Not everything needs to be DSO. Let’s discover where it actually helps.”

### Phase 3 – Co‑create Experiments (Test & Learn)

**Goal:** Improve ways of working through safe, visible experimentation.

*   Propose small, time-bound experiments to evolve specific practices
*   Make experiments opt-in rather than mandated
*   Define success criteria focused on learning, not perfection
*   Encourage multiple teams to try different variants in parallel
*   Include affected people to co-create the experiments

**Artifacts**

*   Experiment proposals in Markdown
*   GitHub issues linked to experiments (hypothesis, observations, outcomes)
*   Short experiment retrospectives

**Mindset**

> “Good enough to try, safe enough to fail.”

### Phase 4 – Stabilize Useful Patterns (Shared Ownership)

**Goal:** Turn proven practices into shared, living standards.

*   Consolidate learnings from experiments into clearer guidance
*   Make explicit what is **recommended**, **optional**, or **context-dependent**
*   Shift ownership of practices from roles to the community
*   Reduce facilitation dependency on a few individuals

**Artifacts**

*   Versioned “ED\&A playbook” sections
*   Clear decision rights and ownership models
*   Explicit handover of facilitation roles

**Mindset**

> “If it works, we all own it.”

### Phase 5 – Continuous Evolution (Living System)

**Goal:** Ensure ways of working evolve with the system, not behind it.

*   Treat ways of working as a product with continuous feedback loops
*   Regularly revisit assumptions as ED\&A, leadership signals, and incentives change
*   Use GitHub as a permanent sensing and learning surface
*   Welcome periodic “reset” discussions when context shifts

**Artifacts**

*   Periodic reflection summaries
*   Archived practices with rationale (“why we stopped this”)
*   New cycles of sense → experiment → stabilize

**Mindset**

> “Our ways of working are never finished—and that’s a strength.”

### What This Is *Not*

*   ❌ A prescriptive DSO rollout
*   ❌ A process handbook written by a few roles
*   ❌ A compliance exercise

### What This Intentionally *Is*

*   ✅ A shared learning system
*   ✅ A co-creation space for ED\&A ways of working
*   ✅ A practical, lived interpretation of DSO in our context
